Katrin always wanted to be strong and lead people, and a few months ago she got that opportunity and used it to the fullest.


However, like most, she wasn't used to and didn't want to see the Phantoms die, especially such brutal, quick, and most importantly senseless deaths.


Katrin gulped, her fingertips trembling.


Yet, it wasn't the first time Katrin saw something like that. One deep breath and a sharp jerk of her head were enough for her to regain her serious, collected expression.


Her cold black eyes stared intently at the archer, then at the bloodstains on the black sand quickly disappearing under the waves.


"Alan, who are those bastards on the towers? The way I see it there's only four of them, right?"


Making a few clicks, Alan nodded with a frustrated face:


'Yes, that's the One-Eyed Serpent Archers, they're very rare Mid-Yellow Monsters, but from the looks of it they strictly follow the fortress's rules and External Artifact's.'


Before Katrin could say anything, Alan continued:


'Oddly enough, this is new information since no one has come here in a very long time, this is the Red Zone after all.'


Katrin's eyebrow went up.


"Hmm? Before Eclipse, someone had a mission in the Fortress of the Lone Serpent? When was that?"


She hadn't counted on this Phantom still being inside, but the latest information about the dangerous Stronghold could save them a lot of lives, it was essential to Eclipse's successful survival.


For some reason, Alan was delayed in answering. Katrin realized that normally this didn't happen.


"Is something wrong?" Katrin was puzzled.



Alan furrowed his eyebrows heavily, scrutinizing the information available to him.


'The problem is that I see that the data on the fortress has been updated in detail several times. That means that as recently as an hour ago, a Phantom was inside and did a lot of work, but... I can't find a mission with the right conditions...''


Then, Alan's eyes went wide as he logged into the Department of Missions and Military roster.


'Wait... The information on this mission is classified. I don't know what kind of Phantom it was or what its purpose was, but... there's no record of the mission having been completed.'



"I see... So that means that this Phantom is still inside, right? That's good, it must be someone strong if it decided to go to Red Zone alone."


However, there was no confirmation of her words from Alan.


It was worth realizing that Alan had seen that such a mission existed, but instead of details and the Phantom's name, he only saw [Unknown] or random characters blocking the information.



Alan muttered as his large, always bright eyes grew darker.


'This Phantom, a few minutes ago denied the military's request to join you, to join other Phantoms to defend the fortress. I have no further information.'


In a heartbeat, Katrin's face contorted in wild anger, her veins swelling hard and her eyes bloodshot.

Furious, Katrin stomped the ground, kicking up dark dust, her coat shaking, sharing her emotions until... she was distracted by footsteps coming from outside:



The second squad, reached the bridge, they were the first since the first squad was lost...



A tall man with short spiky black hair and green eyes stepped forward. His clothes were covered in wide pieces of cloth suitable for locations with strong winds and sandstorms, and a red mask dangled from his chin.


"My name is Viron, I am the leader of the second squad. We have been informed that you are K2W1-Phantom, is that correct?"


Viron didn't know what had made Katrin so angry, neither did the rest of his squad, but they had to act quickly and clearly, ignoring such things.


Realizing that she had a responsibility for the others, that this was her opportunity to prove herself in a real crisis, Katrin paused for a few moments.


Then, she took a deep breath and closed her eyes. Soon, everyone saw her calm as watery black eyes, serene crystals hiding cruelty and strength.


"Yes, that's right. I am Katrin Graeme, I had a solo mission, but as in your case, the sudden Eclipse changed my plans."


Without slowing her pace, Katrin pointed towards the south wall, where the first squad had died.


"There are four deadly monsters on the walls, according to my Operator they won't touch us if we enter through the bridge. This is verified information."


Several Phantoms got pale faces looking at the remains of the first squad. The realization that only one monster did it in a single shot sent chills down their spines.


But, Viron was more resilient than his squad members, he simply nodded.


"Sure, right now, the latest information is flowing through all the squads heading here. However, even if we can be sure that the rules are working thanks to the unknown Phantom..."


Viron's expression became sad for a moment as he clenched his fists tightly. He, like many, wished that this Phantom was with them instead of hiding inside the fortress for unknown reasons.


"What do we do with the Blinding Sylphyre? Do we wait for the others and attack together?"


In response, Katrin stopped him with her hand and stepped forward, her black coat surging upwards, actively shivering in anticipation.


"Don't worry about that. I want you to help the others get here safely, the waves of monsters haven't come yet, but there are more and more enemies around."



Katrin gripped the hilt of her sword tightly confidently stepping onto the bridge, provoking the first guard.


"I'll deal with the Blinding Sylphyre. Right after that, we'll burst through the gate and wait for dawn!"
